Texas Election Infographics

The following infographics depict the voter trends in Texas from 1960-2016. I utilized ArcGis and Adobe Illustrator to create the maps. A big part of what makes this design effective however, is also the layout, which I designed to resemble a timeline so as to tell a story with the data. The final visualizations were put together using Adobe InDesign.

Day of the Dead Infographics

The following infographics were created for a Day of the Dead altar to honor the journalists that were killed in Mexico in 2016. I created these infographics as a collaboration for an article during my internship at UT's Knight Center for Journalism of the Americas. I utilized ArcGis and Adobe Illustrator to create the maps, as well as my own illustration for the Cempasuchil (Marigolds, an important symbol for the DOTD). The final visualizations were put together using Adobe InDesign.

Teen Pregnancy Infographics

The following infographics were created for my capstone project at UT. The visualizations are part of a multimedia package profiling a teen mom. I utilized ArcGis and Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop to create these.

Center for Mexican American Studies

The following designs were submissions for the CMAS Sam Z. Coronado Student Poster Design Scholarship. Both of my designs won second place two years in a row. The 2016, the theme given for the design was "Loteria". My rendition of the classic Mexican game included iconic UT things, like the Tower, Hook'em Horns and others. The theme for 2017 was "Dream", which I portrayed by a hot air balloon that broke the sky to show that the sky is not the limit. Both of these were created with Adobe Illustrator.

Magazine Layout Design

I created the following are designs as mock up magazine spreads. Inspired by my own brothers in the Corps, the magazine is intended for U.S. Marine members and family, hence the name Teufel Hunden. I designed the spreads using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and InDesign.
