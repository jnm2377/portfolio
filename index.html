Hi, I'm Josefina. I'm a front end web developer and designer.

About me

I'm an Austin based web developer with a background in multimedia journalism and design. My interest in web development stemmed from my work in journalism, where I started out designing things for print. Through that, I realized I wanted to design for more than just print, I wanted to be able to build websites. So I learned to do just that.

As a developer and designer, the technologies I work with include:

My other passions in life include: my pitbull baby Gunny (who you can see a painting of in my artwork section), fitness and health and watching GOT, Grey's Anatomy and This is Us (the last two wreck me equally and I am not ashamed).

What I Offer

Discover

My process begins with researching about my project, the users it's intended for, and the purpose and features of the project. I like to really know who I'm designing for, and what their mindset is before I begin coding.

Design

After brainstorming and researching, my next step is to begin wireframing, so that I have a clear vision about what I need for my code. My experience in design allows me to build not only an effective backend, but a beautiful and intuitive front end as well.

Deploy

Finally, after much time spent planning, coding and debugging, we get to the exciting part where I give you a fully functioning web app! My various skills in design and web development help me carry out your ideas to life.

Loteria

Technologies: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery

Deployment: GitHub Pages

A multi-player game that resembles Bingo, designed and created by myself. Players can choose the number of boards they want generated for the game. An algorithm shuffles the images on the board and the deck of cards at the start of each game. The game announces the winner once a player blacks out their board.

Below are some of my favorite graphic design projects. Click on the images to see more.

Texas Election Infographics

The following infographics depict the voter trends in Texas from 1960-2016. I utilized ArcGis and Adobe Illustrator to create the maps. A big part of what makes this data-driven story and its visualizations effective is the layout, which I designed to resemble a timeline. The final layout was put together using Adobe InDesign.

Day of the Dead Infographics

The following infographics were created for a Day of the Dead altar to honor the journalists that were killed in Mexico in 2016. I created these infographics as a collaboration for an article during my internship at UT's Knight Center for Journalism in the Americas. I utilized ArcGis and Adobe Illustrator to create the maps, as well as my own illustration for the Cempasuchil (Marigolds). The final visualizations were put together using Adobe InDesign.

Teen Pregnancy Infographics

The following infographics were created for my capstone project at UT. The visualizations are part of a multimedia package profiling a teen mom. I utilized ArcGis, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop to create these.

Center for Mexican American Studies

The following designs were submissions for the CMAS Sam Z. Coronado Student Poster Design Scholarship. Both of my designs won second place two years in a row. The 2016 theme given for the design was "Loteria". My rendition of the classic Mexican game included iconic UT things, like the Tower, Hook'em Horns and others. The theme for 2017 was "Dream", which I portrayed with a hot air balloon that broke the sky to show that the sky is not the limit. Both of these were created using Adobe Illustrator.

Magazine Layout Design

I created the following designs as mock up magazine spreads, inspired by my own brothers in the Corps. The magazine is intended for U.S. Marine members and family, hence the name Teufel Hunden. I designed the spreads using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and InDesign.
